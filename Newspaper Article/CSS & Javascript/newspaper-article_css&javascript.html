What is E-Sports?

E-Sports, short for electronic sports, refers to organized competitive video gaming at the amateur or professional level.
It involves teams or individuals competing against each other in various video games, often with cash prizes and recognition.

Key Aspects

  1. 1Competitive gaming
  2. Organized tournaments and leagues
  3. Professional teams and players
  4. Cash prizes and sponsorships
  5. Live events and online streaming

Popular eSports Genres

  1. Multiplayer Online Battle Arenas (MOBA)
  2. First-Person Shooters (FPS)
  3. Strategy
  4. Fighting Games
  5. Sports Games

Growth and Popularity

eSports has experienced rapid growth, with:

  1. 500 million+ global audience
  2. $1 billion+ annual revenue
  3. Increasing recognition as a legitimate sport

eSports Ecosystem

  1. Game developers
  2. Tournament organizers
  3. Teams and players
  4. Sponsors and advertisers
  5. Fans and viewers
